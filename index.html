    Frequently Asked Questions

    Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

    To determine if your helmet is old and needs replacement, look for signs of wear, damage, or cracks on the shell or inner padding. Helmets should typically be replaced every 5 years, as the materials can deteriorate over time. Additionally, if your helmet has been involved in an accident, it should be replaced immediately, even if there are no visible signs of damage. Prioritizing safety is crucial to ensure optimal protection while riding.

    Before riding, inspect your bike for any signs of damage or wear. Check tires, brakes, and other components. Inflate tires properly. If everything looks good, take a short test ride to ensure it's safe.

    When riding your bike, always wear a helmet for safety. Obey traffic laws, signals, and road signs. Stay alert, use hand signals, and be visible to motorists. Yield to pedestrians, ride in the same direction as traffic, and avoid distractions. Maintain your bike regularly for optimal performance and safety.
